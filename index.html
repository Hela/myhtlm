    • Cookies

    A cookie is a baked or cooked food that is small, flat and sweet. It usually contains flour, sugar and some type of oil or fat. It may include other ingredients such as raisins, oats, chocolate chips, nuts, etc.

    What is a cookie?

    A "cookie" is a small file placed within your computer when you visit certain web sites which make use of cookies. A cookie is always associated with a specific domain. The cookie file contains specific information and is stored in a designated place on your computer's hard drive. Cookies are transmitted from a web server by way of a browser such as Internet Explorer and Netscape. Cookies can be used, among other things, to identify you when return to the site. The McGraw-Hill Professional Publishing Group's web site is one of many sites that use cookies.
